Brownies

Description

Brownies are a type of baked dessert that are typically made with chocolate and butter or oil, and often includes sugar, eggs, and flour. They are usually square or rectangular in shape and are known for their rich, chocolatey flavor and fudgy, dense texture. Brownies can be served either warm or cold, and they may be topped with frosting, nuts, or chocolate chips. They are often enjoyed as a sweet treat or dessert and are popular at parties and gatherings. There are many variations of brownies, including chocolate chip brownies, mint brownies, and brownies with nuts or other mix-ins.

Ingredients

  • Butter
  • Eggs
  • Vanilla extract
  • Baking soda
  • Cocoa powder
  • Salt
  • All-purpose flour
  • White Sugar

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease and flour an 8-inch square pan.
  2. Melt 1/2 cup butter in a large saucepan. Remove from heat, and stir in sugar, eggs, and 1 teaspoon vanilla. Beat in 1/3 cup cocoa, flour, salt, and baking powder. Spread batter into prepared pan.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven until top is dry and edges have started to pull away from the sides of the pan, about 25 to 30 minutes. Let cool briefly before frosting.

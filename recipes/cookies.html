Cookies

Description

Cookies are small, sweet baked treats that are typically made with flour, sugar, and butter or oil. They can be flavored with a variety of ingredients such as chocolate chips, nuts, and spices. Cookies are often enjoyed as a snack or dessert, and they can be made in a variety of shapes and sizes. They can be soft and chewy, or crispy and crunchy, depending on the recipe and the cooking method. Many people enjoy eating cookies with a glass of milk or a cup of coffee or tea. Cookies are popular all over the world and are enjoyed by people of all ages.

Ingredients

  • Butter
  • Eggs
  • Vanilla extract
  • Baking soda
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Flour
  • Chocolate chips
  • Sugar

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Beat butter, white sugar, and brown sugar with an electric mixer in a large bowl until smooth. Beat in eggs, one at a time, then stir in vanilla. Dissolve baking soda in hot water. Add to batter along with salt. Stir in flour, chocolate chips, and walnuts.
  3. Drop spoonfuls of dough 2 inches apart onto ungreased baking sheets.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until edges are nicely browned, about 10 minutes. Cool on the baking sheets briefly before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.
